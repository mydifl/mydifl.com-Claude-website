Study & Work Abroad

International Programs

Beyond language learning — DIFL prepares you for structured vocational, scholarship, and immigration pathways in Germany, Japan, France, Korea, and beyond. Each program includes full language preparation, documentation guidance, and expert counselling.

🏭
🇩🇪

Ausbildung — Germany

Vocational Training Program

Ausbildung (Duale Ausbildung) is Germany's world-renowned dual vocational training system combining theoretical classroom learning with hands-on employer training. Programs last 2–3.5 years and lead to a recognized professional qualification. Popular sectors include nursing, IT, engineering, hospitality, and logistics.

  • Language requirement: German A2–B1 (DIFL prepares you from A1)
  • Earn while you learn: trainees receive a monthly stipend (€620–€1,000+)
  • Valid work permit and path to permanent residency in Germany
  • High demand for Indian workers — especially in healthcare and engineering
  • DIFL prepares you for Goethe/TELC A1 to B2 certification
  • Visa counselling and documentation support included
⛩️
🇯🇵

MEXT Scholarship — Japan

Japanese Government Scholarship

The MEXT (Monbukagakusho) Scholarship is the Japanese government's fully-funded scholarship for international students — covering tuition, accommodation, and monthly stipend (¥143,000–¥145,000). DIFL's Japanese course prepares you for the JLPT N2/N1 level required for most MEXT applications.

  • Fully funded: tuition + living allowance + airfare
  • Programs: Research Students, Undergraduate, College of Technology
  • Japanese language proficiency is a key selection criterion
  • DIFL prepares JLPT N5 through N1 — all levels covered
  • Application support: SOP, document prep, interview coaching
  • Duration: 1.5–7 years depending on program type
🏫
🇫🇷

Campus France — France

Study in France Program

Campus France is the official French government agency for international student mobility. France hosts over 400,000 international students annually — with one of the highest concentrations of world-ranked universities. French proficiency (DELF B2 or above) is required for most programs, plus TEF/TCF for immigration.

  • Official pathway for studying in French public universities
  • DELF/DALF A1–C2 preparation offered at DIFL
  • TEF Canada / TCF Canada for French immigration
  • Tuition fees significantly lower than UK/US/Australia
  • DIFL covers all levels from beginner to C2 proficiency
  • Student visa and application counselling support
🌸
🇰🇷

GKS — Korean Government Scholarship

Global Korea Scholarship

The Global Korea Scholarship (GKS), also known as KGSP, is the South Korean government's fully funded scholarship program for international students. Korea is rapidly becoming a top study destination — especially in engineering, technology, and K-culture studies. Korean language (TOPIK) is the gateway.

  • Fully funded: tuition, living allowance, airfare, health insurance
  • 1-year Korean language training included at Korean universities
  • TOPIK Level 1–6 — DIFL prepares all levels
  • Partner universities: KAIST, Seoul National University, Yonsei, etc.
  • Undergraduate and postgraduate programs available
  • DIFL provides TOPIK exam preparation and application support
🍁
🇨🇦

Canada Immigration — French (TEF/TCF)

Immigration Language Program

French proficiency is a powerful advantage for Canadian immigration under the Express Entry system. Bilingual candidates (English + French) receive significantly higher CRS scores. DIFL prepares you for TEF Canada and TCF Canada — the two recognized French tests for Canadian PR applications.

  • TEF Canada / TCF Canada preparation — all 4 skills
  • CLB 7+ in French adds up to 50 CRS points in Express Entry
  • Both Quebec (PEQ) and federal immigration streams covered
  • DIFL has 55+ years of French teaching expertise
  • Courses from A1 to C1 — matched to your immigration goals
  • Immigration consultant referral network available
🏥
🇩🇪

Ausbildung Nursing — Germany

Healthcare Vocational Program

Germany faces a critical shortage of healthcare professionals. The Ausbildung nursing track (Pflegeausbildung) is one of the most popular pathways for Indian applicants — combining language certification, credential recognition, and employer placement in German hospitals and care homes.

  • Minimum German level: B1 (DIFL prepares A1 to B2)
  • Stipend during training: €1,000–€1,200/month typical
  • Recognition of Indian nursing credentials by German authorities
  • DIFL has placed multiple students in Bavarian healthcare facilities
  • Goethe Institut and TELC exam preparation included
  • Medical German vocabulary and healthcare communication training
